Casestudy

Design an encrypted system

Zunu is an advanced privacy-preserving software for you and your business.

Ziroh Labs deals with the encryption of sensitive customer information, including personal and financial data. Due to the nature of user business, they were in need of a system that would provide an added layer of security to protect their customers' data. They decided to implement encryption-based software, which would encrypt all data stored on the system, including files, emails, and other sensitive information.

Introduction

The purpose of this case study is to examine the user experience (UX) of encryption-based software and to identify any areas for improvement. The software in question is designed to provide advanced security features for users, including the ability to encrypt files and protect sensitive data from unauthorized access.

‍

‍

Methodology

The research method used in this case study is qualitative in nature, consisting of interviews and usability testing with a group of users who have experience using encryption-based systems. The participants were selected based on their level of experience with encryption and their willingness to provide feedback on the system in question.

‍

Findings

The system should be able to provide users...

  • Get unified access (SINGLE PASSWORD) for multiple clouds.
  • Search for all your files and emails without losing confidentiality. (Requires NO DECRYPTION).
  • Install and get started with any cloud and email platforms in less than 3 minutes.
  • Encryption of data on any device, cloud, and email platform.
  • Guarantees data confidentiality in the event of device loss, theft, and loss of credentials.
  • Makes data hack-proof with multi-factor authentication on any cloud, email platform, and all your devices.
  • Specifies the permissions for, downloading printing, and forwarding files and emails.
  • Audits file and email activity on any device post sharing.
  • Revokes and erases data on devices anytime in the wake of suspected activities.
  • Leverages built-in ransomware and malware protection for your files.
  • Recovers files securely in the event of ransomware attack.
  • Detects and blocks corrupted files from syncing to the cloud with Intelligent Sync.

The Process

The purpose of this case study is to examine the user experience (UX) of encryption-based software and to identify any areas for improvement. The software in question is designed to provide advanced security features for users, including the ability to encrypt files and protect sensitive data from unauthorized access.

Understanding the problem

Before ZIroh Labs even hired me a product designer, all the old products had been implemented  old design way. These products were created without any usability testing and had little consideration for the technical and product limitations on the scope of work.

When the company decided the products will be come under one encryption system, I decided to conducted research interviews with our primary users (Colleagues) to uncover any pain points that they were experiencing with the separate products.‍ My research encompassed:

  • Understanding the user goals and needs
  • Uncovering pain points with the existing user journey
  • Determining the success of the tasks measured

‍

Wireframing the solution

I quickly mocked up some basic wireframes to gather feedback from Product, Engineering and the users on the overall layout and structure of the wizard form. This involved establishing a standardised visual hierarchy and layout for the future wizard component.

  • Reducing the number of steps to minimise time to completion
  • Surfacing mandatory fields and enabling the Next button to show error validation
  • Establishing clearer visual form hierarchy by grouping related fields
  • The lack of a formal wizard component also meant that I had to come up with a standardised styling and UI pattern for future wizards

‍

Developing the designs.

I conducted usability testing sessions with our primary users to validate whether the new designs would solve the problems. I wrote a script including a scenario asking the users the encryption way of securing their data will help of age groups on their devices.

Recommendations:

Based on the findings of this case study, the following recommendations are made for improving the UX of the encryption-based OS:

  • Reduce the time it takes to encrypt and decrypt large files
  • Improve the user interface to make it more intuitive and user-friendly.
  • Provide more detailed documentation and tutorials to assist users in understanding and using the encryption features.

I conducted a UX review of each front-end ticket that was implemented to ensure it was aligned with the designs before it went live.

‍

‍

Results

Overall, the encryption-based OS provides a high level of security for users, but there is room for improvement in terms of user experience. By addressing the issues identified in this case study, the system can be made more user-friendly and easier to use, which will ultimately lead to greater adoption and satisfaction among users.
